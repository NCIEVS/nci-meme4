4.3 Semantic Type Problem Areas


  • QUESTION: How should substances that are not drugs but include route, form, or dose plus the name of the compound get typed ? Examples of this include flea and tick sprays like "14-DAY FLEA AND TICK SPRAY". (Added 6/28/99)
    • ANSWER: These should be typed as Manufactured Object. They should not get the Clinical Drug STY.
  • QUESTION: How should concepts that involve medical devices containing a specified drug ( like Epipens, Intal Inhaler) be typed? (Added 4/23/99)
    • ANSWER: These are typed as both Medical Device and Clinical Drug.
  • QUESTION: How should concepts that involve allergies be typed? (Added 11/10)
    • ANSWER: Allergy to a specific thing should be either typed as Finding or Disease or Syndrome. Allergic reaction, e.g., Arthus phenomenon, should be typed as Pathologic Function.
  • QUESTION: What STY should be given to concepts like "Communicable Disease Control"? (Added 11/3)
    • ANSWER: According to SJN, this type of activity that deals with a population and not just an individual should get the STY of Health Care Activity. The STY of Therapeutic or Preventive Procedure should be used for procedures and/or activities that deal with individuals.
  • QUESTION: What STY should Hospitals be given? (Added 8/26)
    • ANSWER: Hospitals should be typed with Health Care Related Organization and Manufactured Object. This rule comes from Stuart.
  • QUESTION: What STY should concepts involving positive or negative test results be given? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: They should be typed as findings. An example of this type of concept is "Aceto white reaction negative".
      The assignment of the STY's Finding and Laboratory or Test Result were discussed. Finding was to be used when there is a qualitative component to a lab test, such as increased or decreased. Lab/Test Result is used when a substance is present or not present.
  • QUESTION: What should concepts that involve the status of a form or related administrative process be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: These types of concepts should be types as a Functional Concept. An example of this type of concept is "Claim Form Status".
  • QUESTION: What should concepts that concern Facets and/or Surfaces be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: These should be typed as Body Location or Region. An example of this type of concept is "Articular facet of head of fourth rib".
  • QUESTION: What should Forms be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: Forms should be typed as an Intellectual Product and not a Manufactured Object since some forms are electronic now.
  • QUESTION: What should concepts involving Insertion (of) and/or Origin be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: These concepts should be assigned the STY of Body Space or Junction. An example of this type of concept is "Origin of posterior muscle of thigh".
  • QUESTION: What should concepts involving Capsules (not the drug form) be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: Capsules should be typed as Body Part, Organ, or Organ Component. An example of this type of concept is "Capsule of metatarsophalangeal joint of fifth toe".
  • QUESTION: What should Joint be typed as? (Added 8/24)
    • ANSWER: Joints should be typed as Body Space or Junction. An example of this type of concept is "Wrist Joint".
  • QUESTION: Should prescribed food items like Ensure and Clinutren be doubled typed with STYs of Food and Pharmacologic Substance?
    • ANSWER: Per consensus at NLM, these type of products (like those listed under "Enteral+supplement feeds" in Read) should be typed with the single STY of Food.
  • QUESTION: If you have a diagnostic or laboratory procedure that uses some kind of machine, do you double type with:
    Laboratory Procedure & Machine Activity or
    Diagnostic Procedure & Machine Activity?
    • ANSWER: No, just use the correct procedure terminology. For example, Teleradiology (concept_id # 287876) would just be typed with Diagnostic Procedure. Do not include Machine Activity as an STY.
  • QUESTION: What STY should concepts with "complications" in the heading be given?
    • ANSWER: As a general rule, these concepts should be assigned an STY of Pathologic Function.
  • QUESTION: What STY should concepts that involve clerical and administrative procedures be given?
    • ANSWER: These can be typed as Health Care Activity.
  • QUESTION: What STY should ICD/MX codes be given?
    • ANSWER: ICD/MX codes were created to link 'poisoning by" concepts to the causative agent(s). ICD/MX codes are not synonymous with "poisoning by" atoms, they are related and should be merged only with those agents or chemicals causing the problem. STYs for MX codes should come from the Substance hierarchy of the Semantic Network.
  • QUESTION: What STY should DXP(FI) codes be given?
    • ANSWER: DXP(FI) atoms and concepts should receive STY "Finding"/"Sign or Symptom" or "Laboratory or Test Result".
  • QUESTION: What STY should concepts involving effusion be given?
    • ANSWER: These are assigned a STY of Pathologic Function.
  • QUESTION: What STY should hemorrhage be assigned?
    • ANSWER: These are assigned a STY of Pathologic Function, though some could be Disease or Syndrome (e.g., UGI Bleed, or Peptic Ulcer Hemorrhage).
  • QUESTION: How should the Classification STY be used?
    • ANSWER: If you have something like: Bones and Bone Diseases, the Classification STY would be used; General Morphologic Expressions would use Classification; Digestive System in General, would not use Classification.
  • QUESTION: Are drug-induced conditions typed as "Injury or Poisoning"? For example: steroid induced diabetes mellitus, tardive dyskinesia due to neuroleptic drugs. How about other side effects of drugs?
    • ANSWER: No. Side effects are not to be typed as "Injury or Poisoning". On the other hand, drug overdoses can be considered poisonings; and therefore, the "Injury or Poisoning" STY can be assigned to overdose concepts..
  • QUESTION: How does an editor assign STYS to concepts that refer to "toxic effect and "adverse effect"?
    • ANSWER: Both are to be STY'd as injury and poisoning only. They will no longer be double typed as pathologic function and injury or poisoning. Exceptions will be reviewed on an individual basis, with emphasis on the source context when available.
  • QUESTION: When should "Occupation or Discipline" be assigned and when should "Professional or Occupational Group" be assigned?
    • ANSWER:When the concept refers to the individuals working in a certain field, it is assigned the STY of "Professional or Occupational Group". For example, Goldsmith or Gardener or Geneticist. If the concept refers to the field or discipline as a whole, the STY of "Occupation or Discipline" is assigned. For example, Gardening or Genetics.
