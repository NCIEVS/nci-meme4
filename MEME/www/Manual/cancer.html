Editing Carcinoma, Cancer, and Malignant Neoplasms
 
  • Malignant Neoplasms are NOT equal to Carcinomas (the two should NEVER be merged)
  • Carcinomas are NRW than Malignant Neoplasms
  • Cancer may be = Carcinoma
  • Cancer may be = Malignant Neoplasms


   If I have an atom with "cancer" what do I do?
 

  • RESPECT THE SOURCE (note examples may be hypothetical)
    If you have:   Carcinoma of the right toes (PDQ/SY/208-1234)
                              Cancer of the right toes (PDQ/PT/208-1234)

Since PDQ is asserting synonymy between the cancer and carcinoma term (same codes), the PDQ atom for
Cancer of the right toes, means Carcinoma of the right toes.  Remember, we need to edit what we have in the
Metathesaurus from the sources.  If NCI decides to change PDQ for next year, we will make the appropriate changes
and we have ways during a source insertion to detect that codes or meanings may have changed for a given string.
However, until we receive an official updated version from NCI (or any other source) we go with what we have.
 

  • What if the source isn't giving me enough information to tell?
MeSH has:  Cancer of the right toes (MSH2000/EN/D012345)
 I know it is an NRW entry term to Right Toe Neoplasms but there is no further information.
 If a source does not give you enough information re: whether they mean "carcinoma" or "malignant neoplasms"
 you should assume they mean "malignant neoplasms.  In the case above, you will end up with a multi-meaning
 since PDQ means carcinoma, MeSH is taken to mean Malignant Neoplasms.  That is OK.  You should not merge
MeSH with the PDQ atom just because you can't tell what MeSH means by "cancer"
 

MDR has:  1)  Carcinoma of the right toes (MDR/LT/12345345)
                    2)  Malignant Neoplasms of the right toes (MDR/LT/1347583)
                    3)  Cancer of the right toes (MDR/LT/13948935)
 

Again, there is not enough information in MDR to tell what the atom for "cancer" means so it gets merged with
the ones for malignant neoplasms.

Changes NLM is going to make to help avoid further confusion

  • If a concept contains the string "mumble cancer" we will add a PN with either "Malignant Neoplasms of. . ." or "Carcinoma of. . ."
For cases where there is a concept meaning "Malignant neoplasms of . . . " another one meaning "Carcinoma of. . . " , and one of the two concepts has an atom with the word cancer, we will create an MTH/PT atom with the word cancer in the corresponding concept.  Editors should not move MTH/PT atoms for cancer around unless they are sure of
the meaning.  We will try to add atom notes for the cancer terms to help avoid confusion.

It may take us some time to clean up all the existing records and add the appropriate PNs.  Editors should remember that the meaning of the concept can NOT be determined by the concept name alone.  They need to look at all members of the class, the context, and other information found on the concept report.  If a concept has an atom with malignant neoplasms, then that is probably the meaning even if the concept name contains cancer.  If the concept has atoms with carcinoma, then that is probably the meaning.  If a concept contains both atoms for malignant neoplasms and carcinoma, that is probably indication that something is wrong and the concept needs to be cleaned up.
