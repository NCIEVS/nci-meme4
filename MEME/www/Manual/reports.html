Concept Reports


"As of . . . problems/issues" -  These are "canned messages alerting editors to potential problems the engine detects in the concept record; DO NOT assume these are the only problems.  The complete list of possibilities is presented later in this document.

CN# - (Concept Number) - An identifying numerical key for the concept.  It is useful to use this number in communications with humans and computers.

Concept Name - A string chosen to represent the concept as a whole.  The name is selected from the atoms belonging to the concept by the computer.  The decision is based on a simple priority list (the term whose source/termtype is highest on the list wins.)  This list can be found in Appendix B.5 ORDER OF PRECEDENCE OF SOURCE CONCEPT NAMES.

CUI - (Concept Unique Identifier) - This is useful in file maintenance, etc.  It is not usually of concern to Metathesaurus editors.  The CUI appears as the letter C followed by 7 digits.   The unique identifier for the Metathesaurus concept to which a term and string are linked. The
number remains the same across versions of the Metathesaurus irrespective of the term
designated as the preferred name of the concept. This facilitates file maintenance and
management. In the Metathesaurus distribution formats all data elements are linked to the
concept(s) to which they belong. 

approved/not-approved - Not all concepts in the Mid (the entire working database) get included in the version of the Metathesaurus that is released to the public.  After a report is finished all concepts are usually stamped with the editors initials and approved unless otherwise indicated.  Approval status can also be changed for individual concepts in the interface.

IMN - (Internal MeSH Note)- One of the free text fields from M204 MeSH.  These may have generally useful information (e.g., about ET-ET synonymy or drug specifics) or information only of interest for the maintenance of MeSH itself.  Similar to the Meta Notes.

LO/RO/AQ - Editors can ignore these, they come from MeSH204.  LO = Locator Information (what files the concept has appeared in); RO = Record Originator (Who worked on the record in M204 MeSH); AQ - Allowable Qualifiers (What qualifiers can an Indexer use with this record).
