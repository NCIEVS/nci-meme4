LOINC Editing Tips

Logical Observation Identifier Names and Codes (LOINC)
==============================================================================
The LOINC database provides a set of formal names and standardized codes for
identifying laboratory and clinical observations. The purpose is to facilitate
the exchange and pooling of clinical or laboratory results, such as blood
hemoglobin or serum potassium, for clinical care, outcomes management, claims
attachment, and research. 

A fully-specified LOINC name consists of 5 or 6 colon(:)-delimited elements:

    :::::

The component/analyte element of the fully-specified name may have up to 3
subelements separated by carets (^).  The system (sample type/site) element of
the fully-specified name can have up to 2 subelements separated by carets (^).
Refer the LOINC User's Manual for detailed explanations of names and elements
at http://www.regenstrief.org/loinc/loinc.htm

LOINC contains laboratory, clinical, and claims attachment "observation" names.
The Meta LCN attribute denotes whether a LOINC code pertains to laboratory (1),
clinical (2), or claims attachment (3).

===============================================================================

LX          Official Fully Specified Name with EXpanded Abbrevs

CN          Official Component/Analyte Name
CX          Mixed-Case Component/Analyte Name with EXpanded Abbrevs

SN          Official Component/Analyte Synonym
SX          Mixed-Case Component/Analyte Synonym with EXpanded Abbrevs

RN          Official Component/Analyte Related Name
RX          Mixed-Case Component/Analyte Related Name with EXpanded Abbrevs

LS          System/Specimen/Sample Type/Site/Location

Only LN, LO, and LX terms have a LOINC code.  Remaining termgroups are derived
from an related to many LOINC code terms, hence have code=NOCODE.

DEFINITION RN for 
CN:Component@Property@Time_Aspect@System@Scale_Typ@Method_Typ@Class If a LOINC 
record includes a Related Name (RN, in field #8), these related names 
become DEFINITION attributes with the notation "RN for CN:"
with the Fully Specified LOINC name as the attribute value. 

This DEFINITION is intended to help the editors decide what the Related Name 
means. Related Names are used in LOINC primarily to permit matching of
laboratory tests in the RELMA software and may be ambiguous and 
or acronyms. The Fully Specified Name should make the RN
clear.
Specific Editing Tips for Editors:

1. A concept that appears to be a ratio of two chemicals such as, 2-Aminoadipate/Creatine, should be typed as "Quantitative Concept. Aother example of a ratio would be "ALKALINE PHOSPHATASE.OTHER FRACTIONS/ALKALINE PHOSPHATASE.TOTAL".

2. If it appears to be an organism, take it on face value and apply the appropriate Organism STY such as "Plant". 

 CN# 4133664  Alopecurus pratensis
> CUI     Concept Status needs Review
> DEF [Do not release]
>   LNC203/RN|15720-6^FOXTAIL MEADOW AB.IGE.RAST
> CLASS@ACNC@PT@SER@ORD@@Alopecurus
> pratensis;g16@ALLERGY
> ATOMS
>              N []  Alopecurus pratensis [LNC203/RN/NOCODE]
>
> REVIEWED RELATED CONCEPT(S)
> [RT?]  FOXTAIL MEADOW ANTIBODY.IMMUNOGLOBULIN E.RAST CLASS
> [LNC203/CX||LNC203|LNC203] {2766973} S
> [RT?]  FOXTAIL MEADOW ANTIBODY.IMMUNOGLOBULIN E
> [LNC203/CX||LNC203|LNC203]
> {239806} S
3. Rast Classes such as FOXTAIL MEADOW ANTIBODY.IMMUNOGLOBULIN E.RAST CLASS should be typed as "Qualitative Concept".

Something like FOXTAIL MEADOW ANTIBODY.IMMUNOGLOBULIN E is considered an immunoglobulin and should be typed as "Immunologic Factor" & "Amino Acid, Peptide, or Protein".

4. A chemical with a modifier attached (other than FREE) such as, NEUTROPHIL CYTOPLASMIC ANTIBODY.ATYPICAL or ALKALINE PHOSPHATASE.TOTAL, should be typed as "Laboratory or Test Result".

5. A chemical with the word "Free" Attached such as, ANDROSTANOLONE.FREE, should be typed as "Clinical Attribute".

6. All the long strings from LOINC are "Clinical Attributes".

7.  More Hints on Assigining STYs

Organism Attribute: Something that is going to be measured and applies to the organism as a whole or one of it's major parts. Things like height/body weight/body temperature.

Clinical Attribute: Something that is going to be measured and it doesn't apply to the entire organism then it is a clinical attribute. For example, ACCOMIDATION WIDTH. MEASURED. If you aren't sure if something is a "major part"  or for some other reason can't decide between organism attribute and clinical attribute, lean towards using clinical attribute.

Pathologic Function: If the concept includes a finding as a result of a pathologic function, for example Increased Intraocular Pressure, then assign Path Function. Also: Ineffective airway clearance due to inflammation.

Disease or Syndrome: If the concept includes a finding as a result of a disease, then it should be typed as disease or syndrome.  Ineffective airway clearance due to chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.

If you are having trouble deciding between one of the above or a STY has already been assigned that seems reasonable, you should not spend too much time worrying about it or trying to change it.

> {2764820} S
> [RT?]  CARAWAY ANTIBODY.IMMUNOGLOBULIN E
> [LNC203/CX||LNC203|LNC203]
> {4121091} S

Rf265 is a code that corresponds to the Caraway Antibody. IGE. It should be typed as Indicator, Reagent, or Diagnostic Aid, & Amino Acid, Peptide or Protein & Immunologic Factor. You may also see other similar concepts such as
C70 that appear in the same field in the definition. These are also test codes that correspond to antibodies.

9. Anything that is written or created by medical staff like DIALYSIS RECORDS, DISCHARGE NOTES, patient records, etc. should get the STY of "Intellectual Product".
