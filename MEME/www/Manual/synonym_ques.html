Questions on Synonymy in the Metathesaurus


  • QUESTION: Are toxicity and poisoning synonymous in the Metathesaurus?
    • ANSWER: Concepts involving toxicity and poisoning are close to being synonymous but are not 100%. Therefore, do not considered them synonymous unless a source indicates that they are in the context of the concepts.
  • QUESTION: Are sequela and late effects SY?
    • ANSWER: These are similar concepts but are not synonymous. Sequela can refer to short effects.
  • QUESTION: Are drugs and agents synonymous?
    • ANSWER: In most cases, an agent causing adverse effects and a drug of the same name as agent causing adverse effects can be merged together.
  • QUESTION: Can MeSH Publication Types and Main Headings be merged as one concept?
    • ANSWER: Note: As of July 2001, this policy has changed. Publication Types are not to be merged with Main Headings. They will be kept as separate concepts and given PNs.

      Old policy: Publication Types (DC = 2 ) can be merged with Main Headings (DC = 1) when they match lexically and the editors agree that they are naming the same thing. I think the Pub Type distinction is really one of usage, not of meaning.
