Editing Management and Release FAQ - Overview

Between 1990 and 2000, the UMLS was produced in an annual cycle usually in January after the editing was completed by November. Since then, more frequent releases are anticipated; maybe upto four a year.

The editing of the Metathesaurus takes place in the Metathesaurus Information Database or MID which was in an Ingres database till 2000. During that year the MID and all associated programs were ported to Oracle. The schema for the MID differs slightly from that used for the release files. The process of mapping one to the other is known as "production". Once the release files (the MR files) have been made and checked by LTI/Apelon, NLM runs independent Q/A and scripts to convert this to the form that will be pressed on the CD. Then the editing cycle starts over again.

There are systems for source insertion and merging (LTI/Apelon), tools to facilitate the editing: the editing interface (NLM) and tools to generate the workload and manage the editing process: the EMS and WMS (NLM).
